Broken Rice

Broken rice refers to rice grains that have fractured or broken during the milling process. This can occur for various reasons, including mechanical stress during milling, the rice's inherent characteristics, or the milling process itself. Broken rice is a byproduct of rice milling and is categorized based on the size of the broken grains.

Rice Bran

Rice bran is the outer layer of the rice grain that is removed during the milling process to produce white rice. It is the brown, nutrient-rich layer that lies just beneath the outer husk or hull of the rice kernel. Rice bran is often considered a valuable byproduct of rice milling and has various uses and benefits.

Husk

Rice husk, also known as rice hull, is the protective outer layer of the rice grain. It is a hard, inedible layer that surrounds the edible rice kernel. Rice husk is removed during the rice milling process to produce white rice, brown rice, or other rice products
